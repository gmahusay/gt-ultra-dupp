GT Ultra

GT Ultra dances between the worlds of sans and serifs, fusing calligraphy and construction. Achieving a balance between flair and function across a versatile typographic system, the design combines the centuries-old context of serif type with the dynamism of modern sans; challenging its own definition and questioning contemporary typographic expectation.

The world is rife with anonymous sans typefaces, carrying with them the misguided notion that they are contemporary by default. They're slick, they're modern, they're now. Likewise, serifs are often seen as inescapably historical, old-fashioned, and out-of-date.

Well, GT Ultra is here to prove both of those assumptions wrong. It's time for something else; something that objects to what's expected of contemporary typefaces. Something that is neither here nor there.

With energetic architecture and finespun details laying its bedrock, this versatile, variable typographic system utilizes the latest typographic technology to realize its full potential, while showcasing its contrary combination of contexts; informed by the past, affected in the present and crafted for the future.

GT Ultra draws inspiration from the clunky, chunky, and funky flare serif typefaces from the 1970s and '80s, taking cues from their decorative aesthetic to craft something entirely new; a humanist flare sans.

Within these parameters GT Ultra finds its own unique sweet spot. An uncommon space between genres. Something both durable and delicate, something both calligraphic and constructive, and importantly, something both sans and serif. By its very nature, it confronts the preconceptions of both and challenges the idea that humanist flares can't be sans.

Our process, much like the alphabet, begins with the letter a. This single letterform kick-started the journey of GT Ultra's construction, acting as the genesis behind designer Noël Leu's initial sketch of an elegant, traditional, and classically constructed serif. The design then went through a pragmatization of sorts; decluttering the typeface of its decoration and construction.

Noël slowly began to develop the proportions of a sans and provide a more geometric construction to the budding typeface. He did this by removing the serifs and ornamental ball terminals, reducing the optical contrast to a single line width, and compressing the horizontal rhythm.

Introducing a weight axis laid the groundwork for a fully-functioning Standard subfamily, resulting in a sturdier and more obviously constructed skeleton of the typeface. This, however, was only the beginning of GT Ultra's development.

Juggling the task of creating a typeface that is both constructed and hand-crafted, Noël worked to give the existing geometric framework warmth while avoiding the more varied horizontal proportions associated with calligraphy. Noël methodically repeated geometric forms across the typeface, leading to a distinctly constructed feel and character.

The solution to adding contrast without simply re-serifying the typeface lies in the flared terminals. Adding flares to the sans construction went beyond simply adding contrast. This provides a necessary sturdiness to the letterforms.

GT Ultra is a child of process. From angled terminal strokes and bars to diagonal stems, pointed terminals, and flare serifs, the typeface's success comes down to the smallest decisions grounded in context, technicality, and charm.

Noël developed Italic styles equally as charismatic and lively as the upright typeface, having gone through the same process of pragmatization to optimally suit its counterpart. The narrower proportions of the Italic are balanced by its lighter weight and steeper terminal strokes. This allows it to effortlessly blend into both geometric slanted sans and serif italic on an interpolable axis.

Our Ultra cuts push the weight of the design to the absolute max. Each letter of GT Ultra Ultra (hell yeah!) is designed to be as bold as possible, exaggerating the typeface's distinct quirks to create a playful, jubilant, and notably fun version of the upright design. Bursting at the seams, its charm is accentuated by the external removal of flares and its über-tight spacing to maintain the weight's rhythm.
